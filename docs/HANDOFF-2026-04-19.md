**Note:** `metabokg-simulate seed` calls `kinetics_fetch.seed_kinetics()` (human values).
CHO kinetics must be seeded explicitly:
```python
from metabokg.cho_kinetics import seed_cho_kinetics
from metabokg.store import MetaStore
with MetaStore(".metabokg/meta.sqlite") as store:
    n_kp, n_ri = seed_cho_kinetics(store, force=True)
```

## Key Context

**Why Betenbaugh:** Lead PI of AMBIC (Advanced Mammalian Biomanufacturing
Innovation Center) — a NIIMBL-backed consortium with 20+ biopharma member
companies (Pfizer, Merck, Regeneron). He controls the research agenda and has
direct industry ties. An AMBIC license = exposure to the entire consortium.
The Elastic 2.0 license means academic use is free, but any biopharma company
running it internally needs a commercial license.

**The pitch angle:** MetaboKG is 80% of a complete CHO metabolic platform
today. The remaining 20% (amino acid biosynthesis kinetics, mAb coupling, fed-
batch constraints) is exactly where AMBIC flux data would complete the picture.
Data partnership → closes the gaps → joint publication in *Metabolic
Engineering* or *Biotechnology & Bioengineering*.

**KEGG organism codes:**
- `hsa` — Homo sapiens (369 pathways, already in repo)
- `cge` — Cricetulus griseus / CHO (366 pathways, downloading)

**iCHO2441:** Consensus CHO GEM, Hefzi et al. 2016 updated. BioModels ID:
MODEL2206100001. 6,663 reactions, 2,441 genes, 4,456 metabolites. SBML L3V1 —
MetaboKG's SBML parser ingests it directly.

**SABIO-RK API:**
```
https://sabiork.h-its.org/sabioRestWebServices/searchKineticLaws/entryIDs
  ?q=Organism:"Chinese+hamster"
```
Returns ~800 kinetic law entries. Sandboxed environment blocked this; run locally.
